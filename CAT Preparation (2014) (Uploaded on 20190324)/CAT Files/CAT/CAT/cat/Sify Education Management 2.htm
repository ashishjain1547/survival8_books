CAT - Sailing Through Two Stormy Hours - 2
 

By - Divyanshu, IIM Calcutta

The points mentioned in Part 1 of this article bring me to the questions of individual strengths and weaknesses in the different sections. One mantra that I practiced and would like to share with you is stick to your strengths. Almost every serious aspirant has at least one section where he/she feels he might be able to have an edge over others. On the flip side, there are sections that make you edgy. The idea is to stick to your strength while working on your weaknesses. If you can crack one section and clear the cut-offs in other sections, you are sitting pretty!

One problem, which I have seen many people intrigued with, is the tendency to spend too much time on a question. People get into a question and take it up as a challenge to solve the problem. This happens especially in the quantitative ability section. And more often than not, the people who get into this are the people who believe quant is their strength. The challenge in CAT is not to solve the most difficult of questions, but to solve the most number of questions, and solve them correctly so that the score is maximized. Remember, the more you spend time on a single question, chances are, you would end up goofing it up.

Selecting the right questions to solve is more often the clincher in a tight finish. And the best way to do so is to be detached from the individual questions and keep the larger picture in mind. If you feel a particular question can take much time, its better to skip it and move forward, at least during the first pass. When you have finished the first pass through a particular section and then have some time left for that very section, go to the questions you skipped.

One thing that distinguished most successful candidates from the unfortunate ones is the ability to read fast. And this is important not only in the reading comprehension section but in the whole paper. You need time to decide which questions to solve and which to skip. You need time to solve more number of questions. It is always a race against time. And the faster you read, the better it is, quite obviously. So increase your speed of reading and comprehending questions. An ability to read fast coupled with the ability to concentrate for the whole duration is the winning combination more often than not.

The third variable that comes into the above winning equation is accuracy. It is no point spending precious time over solving a question if you cannot solve it right. It is actually worse because you are losing marks. So along with speed, one should take care of accuracy too. Starting from now on, the first thing to do is to increase speed. After a while, speed will saturate to a point. But then, accuracy can get you farther. Normally, speed saturates first and then accuracy levels saturate. The levels at which your speed and accuracy levels saturate are the best indication of the chances you have.

As I stressed earlier, Cat is a race against time. And in this race, the other thing that starts racing is your heartbeats! It is fatal, but quite natural to get charged up, and even nervous during the two hours. After all, this is the very reason I termed them as the two hours of madness!!! But the people who win are the people who keep their cool. Keeping your cool ensures that whatever hard work you have done in the run up to CAT is not wasted and you perform up to your expectations. So to sign it off, I would say, keep your cool.
