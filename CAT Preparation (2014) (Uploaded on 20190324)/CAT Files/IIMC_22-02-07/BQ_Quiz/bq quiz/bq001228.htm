II. BOOK PLAY

Henry Ford almost a century ago said, ``It doesn't matter to me if a man is from Harvard or Sing Sing. We hire the man, not his history.'' Not true any longer, with the college degree carrying as much weight as content, both in managerial jobs as well as the consultant's chair! Match the following famous books on marketing and management with their famous authors. (1 pt each) 

    1. Positioning                               a. Tom Peters & Bob Waterman
2. Managing Brand Equity                     b. Al Ries & Jack Trout
3. The End of Marketing as we Know it        c. David Ogilvy
4. The confessions of an Advertising Man     d. David Aaker
5. In Search of Excellence                   e. Sergio Zyman
