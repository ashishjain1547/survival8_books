Brand Quest      (August 01, 2002)

To get answer, point at the ANSWER icon

I. POT POURRI

A mixed bag of questions on some business related words that are unique to India or had their origins in India. (4 pts each)

  1. Originally a proprietary trademark for a particular model of three-wheeled light vehicle, this name is now commonly used for any light delivery vehicle. The word is also related strongly to a famous Indian business family. What is this word?



  2. What is the Hindi term for the act of surrounding, in the context of labour issues where an official is surrounded by a cordon of workers and shouted down?



  3. A section of the Indian Penal Code, this number has entered common parlance to denote a person commonly considered a 'cheat'. What is this number?



  4. A trailing plant with small glossy green leaves from the philodendron family is widely seen in Indian offices because of the belief that it brings luck. Coupled with this is the belief that the plant brings luck only if has not been bought. By what name is this plant commonly known?



  5. What common two-word phrase is used in India for days on which liquor is not officially sold in the market - public holidays or the pay-day in some states?
