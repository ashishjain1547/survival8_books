I) Banking on brands

Get out your chequebook as you test your knowledge on brands in the banking world. (4 pts each)

  1. What do the three letters ANZ stand for in ANZ Grindlays Bank?



  2. What bank was founded in 1812 as the City Bank of New York until it changed its name in the later part of the 20th century?



  3. Which two banks founded by James Wilson in Britain in 1853 and John Patterson in South Africa in 1862 merged in 1969 to form one of world's largest banks?



  4. What is the name of US President Abraham Lincoln's Treasury Secretary after whom John Thompson named the bank he founded in 1877, as a tribute to his efforts in getting the National Bank Act passed in 1863 and 1864?



  5. Touted as one of the world's biggest bank mergers, this proposed merger between two famous banks with head offices a stone's throw distance from each other finally came a cropper a few weeks back. Name the two banks.



II) BRAND PLAY

Bob Kuperman of Chiat Day once said: ``All great advertising has two things in common. It's effective. And, it's always preceded by a meeting in which someone says, are you crazy?.'' While this does exaggerate the case, marketers are generally prone to taking `safer' or more conventional thinking into building brands. Check out the following talcum powder brands and match them with the companies that market them. (1 pt each) 

            
1. Nycil                                a. Helene Curtis
2. Shower to Shower                     b. Paras Pharma
3. Dermi-Cool                           c. Argus Cosmetics
4. Z                                    d. Johnson & Johnson
5. Premium                              e. Heinz
