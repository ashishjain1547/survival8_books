Brand Quest      (April 20, 2000)

To get answer, point at the ANSWER icon

I. CAR WARS

Find out how tuned in you are to the world of cars. (4 pts each)

  1. Which car introduced recently in India is Japan's largest selling car, and was launched with the slogan `A car full of ideas'?



  2. What's common to the following names: City, Conquest, Celica, Carina and Caldina?



  3. Which company was founded in 1972 as GM Korea _ a 50:50 tie-up between Shinjin Motors and General Motors, US _ and took its new name in 1983?



  4. Not some scathing magazine review, but actual ad copy: ``The Ikon 1.3 falls flat when compared to the ____. Touted as a luxury car, the Ikon 1.3 is surprisingly nothing more than a stripped- down version, which leaves you with a driving experience totally devoid of any luxuries. And that too at a hefty premium.'' Which car brand resorted to this mud-slinging in its ads?



  5. Which famous car sub-brand has a horse as its logo?



II. BRAND PLAY

Leo Burnett once said: ``The greatest thing to be achieved in advertising, in my opinion, is believability, and nothing is more believable than the product itself.'' Brands often focus on advertising without sufficiently differentiating their product. Check out the following toothpaste brands, and match them with the companies that market them. (1 pt each) 

    1. Forhan's                             a. Balsara Hygiene
2. Promise                              b. Geoffrey Manners
3. Aquafresh                            c. Hindustan Lever
4. Pepsodent                            d. Procter & Gamble
5. Crest                                e. SmithKline Beecham
