I. It's a Sony!

It started out in a bombed out department store as Tokyo Tsushin Kogyo (Tokyo Telecommunication Engineering Corp) and today is one of biggest brands in the world. Check your knowledge on the world of Sony. (4 pts each)

  1. Akio Morita was the more famous of the two founders of Sony Corporation. Name the other.


  2. In 1973, Sony was given the Emmy Award for a specific contribution to the television world. What was it?


  3. Sony has exhibited a continuous trend of innovation through its history. In which years did the following innovations take place (a) Introduction of the Walkman (b) Launch of the Sony Playstation (c) Introduction of the world's first pocket transistor?


  4. What product or service of Sony is its money spinner, currently contributing to over 40 per cent of its profits?


  5. In the mid-1980s, Sony lost out in a protracted marketing war over the format of the home video recorder which was won by the VHS format. What was the Sony format called?


II. Baseline Play

In a 1984 book Michael Schudson wrote, ``Advertising is much less powerful than advertisers and critics of advertising claim, and advertising agencies are stabbing in the dark much more than they are practicing precision microsurgery on the public consciousness.'' Very true, considering that most advertising is customer acquisition-oriented while the business requirement may be customer loyalty related! Match the following brands with their baselines. (1 pt each) 

    1. `Connecting people'                              a. France Telecom
2. `Let's build a smarter world'                    b. Lucent Technologies
3. `We make things that make communication work'    c. AT&T
4. `It's all within your reach'                     d. Telstra
5. `Think beyond technology'                        e. Nokia
