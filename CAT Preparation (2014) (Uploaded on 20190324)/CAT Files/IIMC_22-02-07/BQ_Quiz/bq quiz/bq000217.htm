I. DOWN MEMORY LANE

How well do you know the history of ad agencies in India? (4 pts each)

  1. Which was India's first advertising agency and in which year was it set up?



  2. The first foreign agency to set up operations in India was J.Walter Thompson in 1929. Which foreign ad agency originally came in as an exclusive agency for the Unilever operations in India?



  3. Which ad agency was originally set up by Mahindra & Mahindra in 1939, and is currently a division of McCann Erickson?



  4. Which agency was created when the Calcutta branch of D. J. Keymer closed down following the loss of the Dunlop account? (Its employees started another agency with the help of the Government and a few Calcutta companies.)



  5. He founded Rediffusion along with Ajit Balakrishnan and Arun Nanda, and moved on to set up Contract. He later set up another successful agency which merged with Rajiv Agarwal's Nexus Advertising. Name this famous advertising personality and the agency he founded.



II. BASELINE PLAY

David Ogilvy once told the writer Roald Dahl: ``The ability to write well is generally a handicap, partly because most of the customers are uneducated housewives. Most of my copy is awful, by your standards.'' Very true. In many cases, grammar takes a backseat to impact in ad copy. Check out these tourism brand baselines. (1 pt each) 

    1. `Experience the splendours of a kingdom'       a. Singapore
2. `So easy to enjoy, so hard to forget'          b. Malaysia
3. `Always a great place to be'                   c. Bermuda
4. `Just relax'                                   d. Mauritius
5. `99% fun, 1% land'                             e. Thailand
