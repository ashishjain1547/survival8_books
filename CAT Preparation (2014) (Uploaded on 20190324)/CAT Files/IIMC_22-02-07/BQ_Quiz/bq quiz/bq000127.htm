I. CAMPAIGN TIME

How well do you know your ad campaigns? Here are some world- famous ones to test you. (4 pts each)

  1. In a campaign series by BBDO for this famous magazine, the bold copy reads `I never read _____.' Management Trainee. Aged 42. Name the magazine.


  2. What was the famous copy in the Saatchi & Saatchi campaign for the Conservative Party in the UK in the late 1970s, with the baseline `Britain's better off with Conservative'?


  3. Name the brand in this famous campaign by Bill Bernbach: `We are in the rent-a-car business playing second fiddle to a giant. Above all, we've had to learn how to stay alive. No. 2ism is the ___ doctrine. And it works.'


  4. In a classic example of commodity branding, this brand became an overwhelming leader in the salt category with the powerful copy: `When it pours, it rains.' Name the brand.


  5. `Be all you can be' is the big promise held out by an organisation, successfully, in an ad campaign developed by N.W. Ayer & Co. Name the organisation.


    II. BASELINE PLAY

    David Abbot's advice on advertising copy: ``Put yourself into work, use your life to animate your copy. If something moves you, chances are it will touch someone else too.'' Emotion is an important essence of copy, but not found in many advertisements. Check out the baselines of some Internet Service Providers. (1 pt each)
