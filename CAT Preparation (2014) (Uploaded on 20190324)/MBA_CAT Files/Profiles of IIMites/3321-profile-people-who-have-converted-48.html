@All,
Here I go.

Name : Naren N Kumar
CAT 2007 - 94[VA]/95[QA]/95[DI] | Overall - 98.02
Category - General
Calls - K, MDI[All 3], Spjain [Finance]
Converted - K
Rejected - MDI[PGPM], Spjain [Finance]
Waitlist - MDI[HR[5]/PGPIM[84]]

Work-ex
22 months of work-ex in Software development in Oracle.

X - 75%[ICSE]
XII - 78.4%[ISC]
Engg - 79 % [B.E, BMSCE, Information Science]

My interview was only about maths and basketball[my hobby], another question was on my area of work.

Guys I rarely remember getting a AIR in my mocks or for that matter a mock CAT in which I cleared all cut-offs with ease. But what I know for sure is, I never gave up.

I sat in GDs and PIs with BLACKIs and IITians, they are very talented and hard-working people, but hey I am no lesser and neither are you.

I remember reading a similar thread on PG last year and wonder if I'd ever get through an IIM.

The fact is never bother about the goal, just put in the efforts that are required to achieve it, before you'd realise, the goal is yours.

Your marks, achievements, extra-curriculars are all a thing of the past. If you have done well in these, please pat yourself and in case you haven't fared too well, now is the time to mend it.

I read stories of other puys and their struggles, this always helped me undermine any difficulty that I faced.

Stories of Prem Ravi and Siddharth Arora made me realise what extent do people go to realise their dreams.

Regards,
Naren.

P.S : A lone call getter, joining K.

My advice to the puys is that CAT is not a hard exam, its a hyped exam. Have the belief that you can crack it and you will..

Speaking about mocks, everybody requires different kind of needs but one thing is very clear- dont take mock scores too seriously. They are places where you can improve not a place where you need to prove.
There had been times when there were very low scores for me, especially my last mock was my worst with 78 percentile. But still I knew I can do it.

Once you get through written exam, be confident and try to know about yourself.. IIMs want just good people not hi-fi bonds..

All the best to the future junta.
