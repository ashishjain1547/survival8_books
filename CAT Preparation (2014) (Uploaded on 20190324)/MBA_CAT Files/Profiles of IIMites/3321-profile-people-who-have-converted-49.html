Hey puys

I have been going through this thread for quite sometime and I am very happy to finally contribute to it. I had a score of 98.8 in CAT 2006 and had converted MDI-PGPM, gurgaon. But I wanted IIMs hence I decided to take CAT 2007. IT was a tough decision and a gamble, which fortunately paid off.


Name : Akshat Agarwal
Class X: 84.8 % (DPS , vasant kunj, new delhi)
Class XII : 84.2 % (DPS , vassant kunj, new delhi)
BTech: LIT, Nagpur Univeristy ( 78.7%), univesity silver medalist
CAT 2007 - VA : 99.73, DI : 99.69 , QA : 85.27 , overall 99.70
Category - General
Calls - LI, MDI-PGPM, SPJain(Finance),FMS(FT)
Converted - L, I, MDI-PGPM, SPJain (Finance)
Rejected - FMS(FT)

Joining: IIML
I had great interviews everwhere, except FMS, where they werent even listening to me. They had actually decided to chuck our whole group after the GD.
Areas where I scored in the interviews were:

- Good Acads : Apart from being the university silver medalist, I had international publications and had won physics olympiad and international design competitions. They might have noticed this, but I was not asked much on academics in any interview. They asked me on physics in IIML and international design competition at IIMI.

- Music : I am a trained classical music singer and was asked to sing at IIM L, and SPjain. After I sang, the prof at spjain told me that he didnt wanna ask me anything else and that I am through. in my Indore interview, I was asked a lot on rags and effect of music on our body.

Lesson: U shud have one thing that interests them. I had music. I am sure you guys would have something else...

- Work-ex : 23 months of work-ex at ANSYS-Fluent. This worked tremendously in my favour as this company is into computational fluid dynamics (CFD), a research field in core engineering. My work at my firm was also sent for international publications , so that was one area where i scored in the interviews especially indore and MDI.

- Badminton : I was the college badminton team captain during graduation. Was asked some things on it, although the major stress was on music.

I would agree with the posts above, and say that the only thing that matters in CAT is the belief in your abilities and your confidence.

Some luck is involved in the written test, especially in the verbal section, but after that you have to do some soul searching and prepare your answers.
Two most important questions are
- Why MBA ?
- Tell me about yourself?
Answer these properly and you are done.

All the best to the future CAT crackers.

Cheers
Akshat

Cat 2007:99.70
Converts : IIM Lucknow,IIM Indore,SPJain Finance, MDI-PGPM
Reject:FMS 2008 : 99.49 (FT)

Joining IIM Lucknow..........................

Hopefully this helps people:
Acads: Xth - 95.2% (ICSE)
XIIth - 81.8% (Maharashtra H.S.C.)
B.Tech - 8.03/10 (IIT Bombay Metallurgy)
CAT 2007 - 100%ile, (VA 98.93, QA 99.94, DI 99.99)
Calls- BLACKI
Decent extracurrics including winning several national level quizzes etc.

B - Okay interview, until I told them that I didnt like the job at the bank where I was placed which seemed to annoy them. Good GD. Convert
L - Good GD, interview was peaceful. Convert
A- Essay was so-so, not much time to think. Fumbled a lot during the interview and profs seemed to be laughing at my expense. Convert
C - I thought the GD was good until in the PI the profs. said ' You didn't seem to be listening to anyone during the GD.' and 'Why are you here? You don't look serious about this at all.' Convert
K - Decent GD. Bad PI, fumbled with basic vector stuff and was kicking myself after the interview. Convert
I - Didn't attend.

A few points:
1. Never try to evaluate your own interview, especially mid-way through it. I thought I had screwed up A,B,C,K but it seems my parameters for judging myself and the profs. for judging me were completely different.
2. Nowhere was I asked about my acads. The closest anyone ever got was 'Have you studied nanotechnology?' to which I replied 'No.' That simple. Be prepared with plenty of areas of interest.
3. Interesting questions from the C interview: 'Do you have a girlfriend? What does she do?' and 'Tell me all the times in your life that you have failed.'

Best of luck to everyone.
