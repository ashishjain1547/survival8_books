Well... I have been converting IIM calls for two years now... I have the average profile of an IIM candidate... And that has cost me this year...

My Profile:

10th: 89.14% (Gujarat Board)
12th: 78.15% (Gujarat Board)

Engineering (Elex. and Comm.): 7.39/10 (68.9%) (Nirma University)

Extra-Curriculars:
1. Finance Director in ISTE students' chapter in college
2. 3rd prize in national level technical paper presentation competition
3. 1st prize in poem writing in college level competition
4. Was doing an Entrepreneurship Development Institute course this year

Experience:
Cognizant Technology Solutions (2 months at the time of IIM-K interview)

2006:
Calls: FMS, MDI, IIM-L (ABM) (98.79%ile)
Converted: IIM-L (ABM)
Didn't go

2007:
Calls: IIM-ACIKL, MDI, IIFT (99.86%ile)
Converted: IIM-K, MDI


For future call-getters: (This is generic info... Exceptions are there but the percentage is miniscule)
I think profile (either academic record or experience or both) matters in all top institutes, especially
1. IIM-A (though it is not very explicit about it, if you go through the records of people selected, you will find most have very good academic record, or have experience of at least 2 years),
2. IIM-B (as everyone knows, IIM-B prefers people with good academic record... Here even CAT percentiles don't matter unless they are 100)
3. IIM-L (this year they have started giving calls also on this basis... People with 99.79%ile also have not got calls.. Since the PGP at IIM-L is considered the toughest among all IIMs except probably IIM-A)

At IIM-C and K, focus is more on your team skills and general knowledge and logical communication skills than anything else
At IIM-K and I, more questions are asked on acads..

This has been my experience...

To GOD's own Kampus now...

Pranav Awasthi
PGP, IIMK,
Batch of 2010
