My First day at IIMC
 

Anindya Sundar Das is a PGDM student at IIM Calcutta

`Pehla din hai college ka` `Darr lagta hai` Naya daur hai zindagi ka` `Daar lagta hai`

Have you ever seen Hindi movies where the hero travels from the village to the city to find flying gadgets and high rises - and almost meets with an accident???

Something akin happened to me the first time I came to this place. I was awe-struck. First I thought I would get lost in this HUGE campus; then I feared I would drown in those seven lakes; then I thought, `who knows? Maybe I’ll learn to fly`

Well, actually I got lost amidst some of the most wonderful people I’ve met in my life. Intelligent people, humble people, Kung-Fu champions and Masterminds. They were all flocking the avenues getting to know each other.

I got drowned too - not in a tub of beer (what were you thinking?) - in a chasm of solitude in a very special centre called ‘Management Centre for Human Values’. The pink domes standing erect against the green background gave the place a celestial appearance and the statue of Buddha seemed to absorb all the uncertainties of human life.

And the roads were working overtime too. The last few students were trickling in = cursing the railways for running late.

Post dinner, the jetty called on us. And then the Howrah Bridge, and then the ‘Baski’ court, and then The clock was slowly getting past midnight and then one in the morning and then two Who cares? The ‘I’s and ‘me’s were making way for ‘we’s and ‘us’s.

As they say in ad world lingo - a time for friends.

As the night prolonged, a cool breeze swept across the campus cooling off the summer night. A few droplets of summer night rain and the aroma of the wet soil filled the air - Possibly the wind wanted to make new friends - Possibly the raindrops wanted to touch our shoulders - But we decided to store a few good things for the next two years to come and proceeded towards our hostel rooms.

My first day at IIMC was most memorable, space permitting, I can write an entire chapter on it. But by the time I reclined to my bed for a good night’s sleep that day, I knew one thing . I’ve started flapping my wings. The flight is not far away.

Anindya Sundar Das is a PGDM student at IIM Calcutta
