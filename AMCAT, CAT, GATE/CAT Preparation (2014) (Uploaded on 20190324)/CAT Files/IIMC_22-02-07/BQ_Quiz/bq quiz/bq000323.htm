Brand Quest      (March 23, 2000)

To get answer, point at the ANSWER icon

I. Guest appearance

Brands have always cleverly placed themselves in famous movies and not always by chance. They have paid for their special appearance. Can you figure out some of these (4 pts each)

  1. In the 1987 movie Wall Street, Michael Douglas, playing the Wall Street trader Gordon Gecko, waves a magazine proclaiming it The Bible. Which magazine was it?


  2. Paid brand placement in movies probably started after the success of unknown candy brand Reeses Pieces in the mid-1980s when a chance placement in a famous movie increased its sales five-fold. (Incidentally, M&M turned down that opportunity). Name the famous movie and why it was particularly memorable?


  3. Following the release of the movie Risky Business, featuring Tom Cruise, the sales of this brand picked up dramatically, following a clever product placement. Name the brand.


  4. Among successes of the early 1990s was The Saint, a remake of a famous 1960s TV serial. What famous product used by the lead character was launched with this?


  5. The BMW Z3 Roadster was launched as the car of choice of James Bond in the movie Golden Eye. Which make of car was till then the preferred car of James Bond, featured in almost all his earlier movies?
