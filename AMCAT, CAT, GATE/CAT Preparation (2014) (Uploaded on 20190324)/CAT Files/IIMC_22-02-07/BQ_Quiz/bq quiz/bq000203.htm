I. IN THE MAG BAG

Load your magazine and start firing. Check out these mag facts. (4 pts each)

  1. Which magazine was launched in 1922 by Dewitt & Lila Wallace and was promoted extensively by direct mail only, with no mass media advertising at all in the first four decades of its existence?


  2. What famous magazine, founded in 1953 by Hugh Hefner with Marilyn Monroe on the cover, was released without a cover date as the publisher was unsure if there would be a second issue?


  3. Founded in 1967 by Jann Wenner as a representation of youth and hippie culture, it has evolved into a magazine with broader appeal, and uses the famous Perception vs Reality advertising. Name the magazine.


  4. This jeans brand created a sensation with a 116-page insert in the women's magazine Vanity Fair. There were 107 pages with scantily clad models but absolutely no copy in the entire supplement. Name the brand.


  5. In terms of revenue, which organisation is the world's largest magazine publisher?


II. BASELINE PLAY

David Ogilvy once said: ``Ninety-nine percent of advertising doesn't sell much of anything.'' Shocking, but true. Many companies undertake corporate campaigns with one eye on the customer and the other on the stock market _ often with disastrous results. Check out these baselines of auto corporate ads. (1 pt each) 

    1. `Engineering your tomorrows'         a. Mahindra & Mahindra
2. `Tougher than the toughest'          b. Bajaj Tempo
3. `Giving wheels to India'             c. Hero Honda
4. `Leading the way'                    d. Bajaj Auto
5. `Value for money, for years'         e. Ashok Leyland
