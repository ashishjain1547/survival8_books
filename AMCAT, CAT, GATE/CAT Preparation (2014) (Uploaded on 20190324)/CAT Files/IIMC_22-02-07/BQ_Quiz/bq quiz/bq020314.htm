I. BUZZWORDS

Check your knowledge on the latest buzzwords in the world of business. (4 pts each)

  1. A marketing term drawn from the agricultural world, this phrase describes the process of slowly building a customer's file, never asking for too much of their private information at one time. What is this term?



  2. When you describe a person as '404', what do you mean?



  3. The consumer response to recessionary times is described as the 'lipstick effect' . Describe what this actually means.



  4. What are 'silos'?



  5. The latest in performance evaluation jargon, this involves obtaining an assessment from everyone involved - subordinates, superiors, colleagues and customers. What is it called?



II. MATCH PLAY

Many international brands are synonymous with the category they are present in, but are not popular in India since they have not officially been launched here. Match the following pioneering brands with the product categories they represent. (1 p t each) 

    1.  Leica                       a. Condoms
2.  Calloway                    b. Cameras
3.  Trojan                      c. Golf equipment
4.  Zeiss                          d. Guns
5.  Winchester          e. Binoculars



III. COPY TEST

Following are words picked out of the body copy of a print ad, voice-over of a TVC or simply a baseline. Identify the brand. (3 pts each)
  1. 'Just exactly how precious your package is to us' (visual of a courier man holding a baby)



  2. 'The first ten minutes when the batsman is under stress - when the engine is under stress'



  3. 'Have you been looking at life upside down? Get the right perspective with the LifeMaker'



  4. 'Customers are like falling hair. If you knew why they were going away, you'd know how to make them stay'



  5. 'Now you don't have a car. Now you do'
